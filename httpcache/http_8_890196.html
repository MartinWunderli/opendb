Sex & Nudity

3/10

Kissing scene. Guy removes shirt while kissing continues. Suggestive to a sexual situation. This intimate relationship also has an important role in the story, because the male transfers some alien substance to the female, making her pregnant with an alien.

As in the first Alien film, when the crew wake from cryosleep they are in their underwear. At various points in the film, a female character wears this kind of cryosleep underwear.

A man asks if a woman is there to get laid. She responds negatively but ends up suggesting that the man should be in her room in ten minutes. We don't know if the two have sex after this. The matter is not mentioned again.

At one point in the movie Elizabeth Shaw (Noomi Rapace) takes off her clothes down to undergarments resembling ace bandages covering explicit body parts to enter a medical-pod which operates on her abdomen. There are many angles. No full nudity but could be seen as inappropriate to some viewers

Shortly after the medical-pod incident Noomi Rapace is seen stumbling around the ship wearing the now bloody ace-bandage under garments, also many camera angles including a close up of her waist and thigh area. Again, no nudity but could be seen as inappropriate to some viewers

Violence & Gore

8/10

WARNING- POTENTIAL SPOILERS AHEAD.

An alien is removed from a woman's abdomen via automated caesarean . We see a laser graphically slice through her flesh, a claw remove the alien sac and then the wound is stapled shut. At one point the alien sac bursts, causing a blood-like liquid to explode everywhere, at which point the woman reaches inside the wound and detaches the umbilical cord. Although there is little blood, the scene is very realistic and gory.

A snake-like creature wraps around a man's arm and snaps it (we see bone poke through his sleeve). The creature then lunges into the man's mouth, which chokes him to death. Another man tries to help him by cutting the creature in half, however it sprays acid at the man, causing his space helmet to graphically melt onto his face.

A human-like alien drinks a black liquid that causes him to writhe in pain. Black veins appear all over his body and he begins to graphically disintegrate, before falling off of a cliff into some water below.

A mutated man jumps on another man's abdomen, crushing it and causing the man to spit up blood. He also smashes another man's head into the ground, causing bloody to spray. He then kills several other people by beating them and throwing them against objects. The mutated man is shot several times and then run over by a truck.

A human-like alien fights with another squid-like alien. They are both killed during the fight. Later, an creature bursts out of the human-like alien's chest, which is shown quite graphically with some gore.

A man becomes very ill, and is set on fire with a flamethrower. He is seen burning whilst still alive for quite a while.

A highly-realistic android is decapitated by an alien. The android's head is then used to bludgeon an elderly man in head, killing him (a large bruise and blood is shown).

A severed alien head is reanimated, an later gorily explodes. This scene is quite disturbing.

Several people fly a spaceship into a large ship, resulting in a large explosion. All people on-board are presumably killed.

A huge spaceship crashes to the ground, as two women try to run away. One woman falls and is crushed off-screen.

A woman is caught in a huge sandstorm, and is nearly swept away.

A man has a badly bloodshot eye, and when he looks in a mirror, he sees a small creature wriggling in it.

An alien throws two people at a wall, presumably killing them.

A woman hits two people with a metal bar, knocking them unconscious.

A woman slams an android against the wall during an argument.

Profanity

Three uses of fuck (one is obscured by static) and some milder language.

Alcohol/Drugs/Smoking

Vodka and other alcoholic drinks are shown being served and consumed aboard the ship.

A man drinks alcohol from a bottle in a couple of scenes.

A man smokes tobacco through a pipe in his spacesuit.

Frightening/Intense Scenes

7/10

Very intense moments throughout the film.

The "engineer" who is seen in the later part of the film is akin to a superhuman. He is violent and aggressive.

The caesarean scene is the most disturbing, and may frighten people of all ages, speacially those who are claustrophobic, afraid of machines, afraid of surgery (presumably with little anesthesics aid for that scene) and giving birth.

15+
